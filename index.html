Executive Summary

Travelling the world is, for many people, an overwhelming experience. When we visit another country, we are thrown into a brand new mix of cultures and etiquette.

We experienced this ourselves on our trip to the USA this Easter, with the majority of students on the trip being under 21 and many not having been across the Atlantic before, the change in scenery was most definitely a culture shock. We found that a lot of the time we felt clueless, having to ask how much a dime was worth or attempting to understand the tipping system. However, even more of a problem was the lack of knowledge aboub what to do – as legal adults in the UK we are used to being allowed everywhere, but as under-21s we found that there was no easy compilation of what to do.

So, TourIt was born. We decided that the best solution would not be just a product to find age-specific activities but also a complete guide to travel that could help in every way possible. Deciding that the best interface for a travel guide would be portable, we settled on an app with a chatbot interface but also explore options that allow the user freedom to ask questions or simply see what’s on. Within the TourIt app is anything a tourist could possibly need, from currency guides and travel suggestions to event price comparisons and language help.

We believe that TourIt will allow travellers from all walks of life to experience the world in a way personalised to them, with less time worrying and more time exploring. Let’s help people experience travel in the best way they can.

  • Written by Freyja Barnard

Technical Specification

Workings:

  • Gathers user data e.g. age, location, interests. Changes language and gives suggestions accordingly.
  • Main interface will include a chatbot that detects certain phrases and words to determine a suitable response.
  • Pulls data from a range of sources to provide relevant information.
  • Also pulls said data when user is browsing sections instead of using the chatbot.
  • Uses location services to determine user’s current location.

Requirements:

  • Smart phone with either an Android or iOS operating system.
  • Ability to connect to Wi-Fi and GPS tracking services. Mobile data desirable but not necessary.
  • Minimum 2GB of free storage space to allow access to the app offline.
  • Touchscreen to accommodate chatbot and app interface.
  • May require other apps to be installed (such as Google Maps).

Data Sources:

  • Uses Google Maps and/or CityMapper for directions and locations.
  • Uses weather data from OpenWeatherMaps.
  • Option to link with online banking (features such as currency conversion and budgeting)
  • Uses online information sources (e.g. government guides) for cultural information.
  • Uses Google Translate for useful phrases.
  • Uses Ticketmaster, Twickets, and SeeTickets for purchasing of event tickets.
  • Uses TripAdvisor for restaurants/bars and potentially the booking process of future trips.

Scenarios of use:

  • Elderly couple planning to visit new country, they are wary of how cultural difference may affect them. The app suggests proper etiquette when interacting with strangers and also suggests some local events. The couple buy tickets at the lowest price for a play so they are well prepared before they travel.
  • A student takes a gap year to travel. They can use the app to plan and keep track of where they go, as well as guiding them through different cultural norms and potentially stressful situations on their travels.
  • A family is currently in the USA; they are struggling for things to do. The app suggests age appropriate events and amenities, taking into consideration the local age limit laws.
  • Written By Lisa Norcross
